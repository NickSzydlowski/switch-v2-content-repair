5-Fold Symmetry

Koruga's views are fascinating. He ties the 5-fold symmetrical structure of Fullerenes and Bio-Fullerenes to the ancient belief systems of the Greeks, Chinese, Indians, and Egyptians, showing how each of them relates. The mathematics and chemistry discussed in Fullerene C60 is far beyond my immediate comprehension, but I do get an intuitive grasp of what is Koruga is attempting to get across.

I am intrigued by what Koruga calls a "synergy of art and science." He indicates a particular interest in the relationship of art and new technology based upon 5-fold symmetry and has worked with artists in this regard.

The Golden Mean

In looking back over art historical instances, there are a number of approaches to artistic representation using Golden Mean, divine proportion, and geometrical and mathematical models. It is interesting to note that "children instinctively represent things they see in geometrical symbols" (Encyclopedia Britannica).

The Golden Mean (or Section) was defined by Lucas Pacioli, during the Renaissance in his treatise Divina proportione. His ideas influenced Leonardo and Durer. Neo-impressionists were intrigued by mathematical models as well as the Cubists, who defined their representation of the world through this mathematical system of proportion, even naming their exhibition Section d'Or (The Golden Section).

Who knows if the obsession Cezanne had with nature didn't originate with an intuitive knowledge of the basic 5-fold symmetry of life, as seen in Koruga's mind synergy -- the unconscious and the conscious coming together?
