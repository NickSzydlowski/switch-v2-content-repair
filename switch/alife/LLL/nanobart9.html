Atomic Resolution


In 1992, on a visit to the U.S., Koruga obtained samples of C60 from Huffman's lab and from the Materials and Electrochemical Research Corporation (MER) which holds the license to the Huffman-Kratschmer patent. He sent these samples via DHL to his associates in Belgrade, who sent him back STM scan images. Koruga stated:

"They were fantastic--on one you could see clearly energy concentrated in pentagons; on another, clear images of 5 and 6 member rings of carbon atoms (or orbitals); while the third showed the Ih symmetrical basis of C60....It was the first time the human eye had looked at the molecular structure of a new molecule with atomic resolution."

Out of this came a proposal for cooperative research between MER and Koruga/Hammeroff.
