Magic Number


The discovery of buckyballs helped to focus a number of chemists' ideas and research, worldwide. It led one research team Huffman/Kratshmer, who had in 1983 produced a "graphite smoke" in trying to solve a problem in astrophysics, to realize what the "junk" they were dealing with in the smoke was C60. Huffman had produced C60 molecules probably as early as 1973, but it wasn't until he and Kratshmer read a paper by Smalley and Kroto in 1985 that they realized what they had, and 1989 until they actually proved it. What makes the Huffman discovery particularly interesting has to do with his blind mother's intuitive feelings about her son. She knew that her son would discover "something important based on the number 60." Being blind, she counted her steps and her "magic number" was 60. Huffman and Kratschmer have gone on to to produce this solid form of carbon C60 calling it Fullerite and have submitted a patent for the production of Fullerenes.
