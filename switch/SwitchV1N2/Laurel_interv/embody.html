Disembodiment vs. Preserving a Physical Integrity



JC: There are many models of experience available on which to base VR, like the competitive-aggressive one. Would you elaborate upon these other models of interaction and talk about what has not been accounted for?

BL: I would put them in two basic sets. One says that VR is 'out of body,' an approach that takes on a couple of different flavors. For instance, the techno-ubiquitous fantasy of 'I'm everywhere' omniscence, the classic super-hero stuff. There's also a fairly big, serious group looking at that as a spiritual opportunity. There was a piece done at MIT about the arctic, wolves and other animals, with lots of interesting p.o.v.'s. Some of them wouldn't be possible for any kind of body that I could construct with my imagination, yet would have enough characteristics of a bodied experience to really involve you. The guy who built it is looking for a disembodied medium.

The other major group, which I consider myself to be a part of, says: "Wait a minute -- the idea of having a medium that attempts to replicate a sensorium which is disembodied is a non-sequitor." But there's a politics under that for me.
