Performative Aspects of VR

BL: In a classical VR context, not a 'mud' VR environment, the idea of walking an avatar around for someone else to see and respond to in a special way, emphasizes the performative aspects of gender. There may be some change of consciousness in people's minds of what gender is. I think it's likely, as has been true in the text-based world, that it is increasingly the case that gender is de-emphasized as a trait that we communicate socially. My guess is that this is because identifying oneself as this or that gender, either by coded representation or by labeling, brings up a whole lot of predictable behavior. Often what we're after when we design an avatar is an experience that is stimulating and invigorating.
