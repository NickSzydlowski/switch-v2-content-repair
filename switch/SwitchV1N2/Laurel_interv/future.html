Conclusion: VR and the Future of Our Relationship with Computers

JC: Would you like to say anything in closing?

BL: Yes. The main point of the Scientific American article was that I think VR holds great promise for changing the ethos of computing. That is, our sense of what computers are, what they're 'like,' and what they have to do with us. My favorite metaphor for this is that computers have sort of evolved as this race of severed heads, brains-in-boxes. That is the way interfaces have worked and been treated for quite some time.

JC: If even that good...

BL: Right. So to say, 'This is a whole body deal,' suggests that what's going on with the body is just as important as what is going on with the brain. That's an enormously important thing here in the twentieth century, here at the end of Descartes' 'little train.' We've gone the whole distance with mind-body dualism and it's time to re-examine this. Our mistake number one: to state that our minds and bodies are separate. Mistake number two: to state that these little brains-in-boxes can take care of just about anything.
