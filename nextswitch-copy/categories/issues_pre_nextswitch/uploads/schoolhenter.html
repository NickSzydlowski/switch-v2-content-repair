Q1 Would you describe your program and its history? How your new media art endeavor's positioned within the academic structure at your University?

The CADRE Laboratory for New Media is a unique research program dedicated to experimental applications of new information technology in the arts. The program began in 1984 resulting from an educational initiative and national conference on art and technology hosted by San Jose State University. In 1985 the School of Art and Design committed itself to establishing a state-of-the-art facility for experimentation and research in digital media. In the late 1980's CADRE hosted an additional three-conferences/art festivals of national stature that provided a forum for the maturing artistic discourses of art and technology. In 1991 Joel Slayton was appointed Director of CADRE.

CADRE's Digital Media Art Program emphasizes theoretical and critical discourse, providing a context in which experimental and scholarly activities are defined. The San Jose State University academic environment is informed by the unique opportunities afforded to a metropolitan university located in Silicon Valley. CADRE intentionally reflects the research culture, orientation and dynamics of the immediate computing and electronics industry. CADRE is strictly a fine art program. Current activity areas include computer visualization, cross-media installation, digital video, interactive and immersive systems, programming, information mapping and database visualization, virtual reality and net art. Next year CADRE embarks on two new ventures; wireless communications and biotechnology. CADRE special programs support a Visiting Artists/Theorist Program, Annual Student Symposium, sponsorship of digital media exhibitions and the publishing of SWITCH, CADRE's internationally renowned net journal dedicated to critical and theoretical discourse. Complimentary study programs are available in Electro-Acoustics, Interactive Literature, Engineering, Computer Science, Theatre Arts, Library Science and a variety of other disciplines.

 

Q2 What degrees do you offer?

The CADRE Laboratory for New Media offers BFA, MA and MFA degrees in Digital Media Art. Programs of study emphasize artistic experimentation within a context of historical, cultural and theoretical study. The program is intended for artists with a multi-disciplinary orientation who intend to specialize in new media and who have a strong interest in contemporary theory and research.

 

Q3 Computers and networks are profoundly conceptual media in the context of literature and the arts. Yet there is also a seemingly unavoidable necessity for students to learn technology skills in a constantly changing technological environment. This seems to demand both a very theoretical/art-historical approach to teaching as well as a need for high-tech education. Does your program have a specific approach to pedagogy in new media arts, especially as pertains to the balance or blending of theoretical and technical instruction?

The quest for discovering a pedagogy that serves to balance the technical and conceptual is doomed to failure. Such an orientation merely lends itself to the modernist tendency of technological advocacy and positions of techno-infatuation. The 'unavoidable necessity' is for students to unlearn the 'technology-skills' to which they have been indoctrinated, requiring pedagogy of confrontation and anti-romanticism. CADRE assumes a world of theoretical contradiction and malfunction. In such a world, one cannot conceptually advance or retract particular hardware/software any more than one can' balance or blend' a pertinent art history of new media. Neither technology nor history are that simple.

Study at CADRE emphasizes a confrontation with art through the development of systems of interpolation and transposition involving the histories of art, science and culture. When this is done from an informed perspective, that is, from an inspired theoretical and philosophical position, the results are often provocative and result in hybridized conditions of art. In which case, specific skills are not at all arbitrary but neither are they completely essential. The goal at CADRE is to manifest a conceptual coherence in the selection of information systems with which to be engaged and what one has to know about such systems to be engaged.

 

Q4 Theorists and practicing artists approach new media and network media from a wide range of interdisciplinary perspectives. Film and cinema theory, communications studies, literature, linguistics and semiology, fine art, activist art, computer science, information science, engineering, philosophy, biology, and other fields are platforms of theory and practice which have influenced new media and/or technology art. Which interdisciplinary approaches are most influential in your program, and how is your program integrated with other departments or programs at your institution?

Art does not require a platform outside of itself. It does require to be informed, both by itself and by its context. This requirement reflects not so much of an interdisciplinary character but rather the importance of art as an insertionary function. Which, of course, can be defined as a complimentary relationship or otherwise. In either case, a most significant factor is the nature of approach. In consideration of new formations of author, reader, participant and process, new media art practice begs the issue of how to determine the necessary circumstance enabling artistic function and appreciation. Art as insertion is something other than observation and commentary, it emerges as its own perturbance in the system of how art can function.

CADRE students are encouraged to situate themselves within intellectual domains of pertinent research. Curriculum in the undergraduate and graduate programs requires cross-disciplinary study at an advanced academic level. Students are encouraged to customize their education by forming specific relationships with other programs and faculty across campus. Contemporary discourses in the information sciences, cognitive sciences, physics, biology and philosophy are of critical issue. CADRE desires to enhance its expertise with regard to art as non-linear and self-organizing system, art as network, art as database, art as inferencing and reasoning system, art as simulation and art as new media. CADRE remains appraised of developments in popular culture including new models of art as business, fashion and entertainment.

 

Q5 How does your program engage with and relate to the traditional institutional artworld of museums and other art institutions?

Providing opportunities for students to engage the art community on multiple levels is central to our mission. CADRE is directly involved with regional, national and international events including participation of faculty and students in conferences and exhibitions such as Siggraph, ISEA and Ars Electronica. As an organization, CADRE sponsors numerous regional symposia and exhibitions featuring new media including this year's conference simulation The Chemistry of Fear Symposium and previous years' topic specific events Chic Tek: Women Artist Defining Technology and CADRE VR Symposium. CADRE's new Visiting Artists Program has brought the likes of David Ross, Steve Dietz, Manuel DeLanda and Lev Manovich to campus for workshops, lectures and informal interaction with students.

SWITCH is produced by graduate students in the CADRE Digital Media Art Program. It has grown as an important informational resource featuring substantial discourse and critical theory. Published three times a year, SWITCH has addressed a range of topics including Net Culture, Artificial Life, Art and the Military, Sound Culture, Cyber-Feminism, Art as Network and Art as Database. SWITCH provides opportunities for original authorship, interaction with world class artists and theoreticians, innovation in design and management experiences.

CADRE site specific media events DoWhatDo, Conduits, Landscape Painting as Counter Surveillance of Area 51, Rocket Launch at Lucky Jim Wash and The Chemistry of Fear have received significant critical attention.

 

Q6 Do you receive industry support or maintain relationships with companies who are interested in your graduates?

Over the last decade CADRE has generated over 2.5 million dollars in hardware, software and network resources supporting state of the art laboratories for experimentation.

 

Q7 Can you describe the type of studio work that your digital media students are primarily engaged in, and is theory and writing an important component of your program?

Study in the arts at CADRE is conceived of as a research process in which experimentation with new media plays a very important role. Students are encouraged to engage contemporary literature including art criticism, philosophy, science and cultural theory. Articulation of views is demonstrated through written and oral discourse.

 

Q8 Is there anything that is important for prospective students to understand about your program that may assist them in choosing a place of study?

The CADRE Laboratory for New Media is first and foremost a community. A community that the educational program is designed to facilitate and promote. Collaboration is an essential element in all enterprise and is at the heart of the identity of CADRE's faculty and student body.

CADRE is a fine art program. We are only interested in attracting students who have a serious commitment to excellence and are interested in issues represented by the convergence of art, science and technology, a convergence situated within domains of popular culture.
